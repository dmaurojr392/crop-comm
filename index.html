COCONUT PRODUCTION IN
AURORA PROVINCE

Importance of Coconut in Aurora

  • Major Crop: Coconut is a primary agricultural product, providing livelihood to many farmers in Aurora.
  • Economic Contribution: The coconut industry significantly contributes to the province's economy through copra (dried coconut meat used for oil production), fresh coconuts, and byproducts like coconut husks and shells.
  • Support Programs: The Philippine Coconut Authority (PCA) often implements programs in Aurora to boost production, such as replanting, fertilization, and pest control initiatives.

Challenges in Coconut Production

  • Aging Trees: Many coconut trees in Aurora are old and less productive, requiring replanting with hybrid varieties.
  • Natural Disasters: The province is prone to typhoons that can damage coconut plantations.
  • Pests and Diseases: Infestations like coconut scale insects have affected production in the past.

Potential for Development

  • Value-Added Products: Aurora has opportunities to develop industries for coconut byproducts, such as virgin coconut oil (VCO), coir (fiber), and activated carbon.
  • Agro-Tourism: Promoting the coconut industry through agro-tourism and eco-tourism initiatives could diversify the province's economic activities.
