Activities

Smart Sensor Network and Technology Synchronization

Mapping the locations of pre-existing sensor networks within Region III and pinpointing the coordinates of data availability for soil moisture sensors, including Agromet stations, operated by State Universities and College (SUCs) and Local Government Units (LGUs). In instances where there are no existing sensors to retrieve data from in a certain location, the Department of Science and Technology Region III (DOST III) will work on establishing a sensor network, potentially through its GIA (Grants-in-Aid) project, to synchronize data and reference points uniformly in the map.

Development of a Data Analytics Platform

After identifying and structuring the sources of valuable data from sensors and different existing smart technologies, the next step involves the creation of a Data Analytics Platform. This phase encompasses the integration of essential technical components required for the project, including the establishment of a Data warehouse, the selection of appropriate data techniques, and the assembly of an expert team. The team's primary role is to provide substantial insights and recommendations regarding the development of the data analytics platform, enabling the conversion of raw data into useful information.

Development of Commodities Analytics System

Data related to commodity information will be mined from various sources and will be filtered before storing to the data warehouse. Subsequently, the data will undergo a comprehensive analysis through a designed desired data analytics model; with a focus on key performance indicators (KPIs) such as crop commodity distribution in an area, agroclimatic conditions, along with its distinct agroeconomic indicators. An online dashboard will then be developed to present these KPIs, along with visual, easy to understand reports generated through descriptive analytics. The data analytics model will undergo continuous improvement throughout the project's duration.

Establishing a Commodity Forecast Analytics System

Incorporating the dataset results from both Activity 1 (comprising data derived from sensors and identified existing technologies within Region III) and Activity 3 (comprising data on the conducive environment for a given commodity and its performance in terms of agroeconomic contribution) a data analytics model will be developed integrating both datasets to come up with a commodity forecast. Prescriptive and predictive analytics will be performed to offer advisory, and commodity forecasts, whereas AI algorithms will be utilized to come up with crop-specific recommendations.
