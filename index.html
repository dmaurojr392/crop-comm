Overview

The project intends to enhance the sustainability of agriculture in Central Luzon by providing deeper description and insights for different commodities. Starting with the top three commodities in terms of the area planted or from the PSA data, which will be identified depending upon its impact and production on the region, metrics such as the crop maps found on Farmers Guide Map/National Color Coded Guided Agricultural Map, Project SARAI etc. will serve as an information source in providing crop advisories/recommendations to farmers and other stakeholders. Machine learning will be utilized for prediction and data analytics for forecast and future trends and tendencies which will be very useful for policy formulation for relevant programs, projects and activities of individual concerned agencies.


Raw data of the identified technologies will be converted into a descriptive insight. Commodities past and present trends will be illustrated through the utilization of descriptive analytics, commodities’ future trends will be illustrated through the use of predictive analytics. The project aims to provide smart commodity forecasts and advisory for farmers to enhance smart decisions to ensure maximum profit and commodity value. Smart commodity forecasts will be made through prescriptive analytics.


The suitability map will provide the farmers and economic planners a bird’s eye view of the commodity/crops capability to thrive and acquire value in each specific place. Recommendations and suitability on maps will not only depend on the existing agroclimatic condition of the area but will be combined with the agroeconomic indicators of the commodity such as the Rate of Return (RR) and probability Index (P). To ensure not only the production but also the value of the produce, profit to farmers and the agroeconomic contribution it will give to enhance the development of the Region.

Activities

Data Gathering

Data Gathering

The project team works closely with the Provincial Agriculture Office in each province of Central Luzon. This collaboration ensures accurate and relevant data collection. Establishing strong communication at this stage lays the foundation for effective system development. It helps align the system's goals with the actual needs of each province. This initial activity is crucial to building a reliable and responsive system.

Development of a Data Analytics Platform

Development of a Data Analytics Platform

After identifying and structuring the sources of valuable data from sensors and different existing smart technologies, the next step involves the creation of a Data Analytics Platform. This phase encompasses the integration of essential technical components required for the project, including the establishment of a Data warehouse, the selection of appropriate data techniques, and the assembly of an expert team. The team's primary role is to provide substantial insights and recommendations regarding the development of the data analytics platform, enabling the conversion of raw data into useful information.

Development of Commodities Analytics System

Development of Commodities Analytics System

Data related to commodity information will be mined from various sources and will be filtered before storing to the data warehouse. Subsequently, the data will undergo a comprehensive analysis through a designed desired data analytics model; with a focus on key performance indicators (KPIs) such as crop commodity distribution in an area, agroclimatic conditions, along with its distinct agroeconomic indicators. An online dashboard will then be developed to present these KPIs, along with visual, easy to understand reports generated through descriptive analytics. The data analytics model will undergo continuous improvement throughout the project's duration.

Establishing a Forecast Analytics System

Establishing a Forecast Analytics System

Incorporating the dataset results from both Activity 1 (comprising data derived from sensors and identified existing technologies within Region III) and Activity 3 (comprising data on the conducive environment for a given commodity and its performance in terms of agroeconomic contribution) a data analytics model will be developed integrating both datasets to come up with a commodity forecast. Prescriptive and predictive analytics will be performed to offer advisory, and commodity forecasts, whereas AI algorithms will be utilized to come up with crop-specific recommendations.
